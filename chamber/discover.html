Your Visit History

Our City

The city boasts a vibrant cultural scene, with influences from both North and South India. It is renowned for its cuisine, which includes iconic dishes such as Hyderabadi biryani and Haleem. Hyderabad is also known for its festivals, including Diwali, Eid, and Bonalu.

History of Hayderabad

Hyderabad has a rich historical background, having been ruled by various dynasties including the Kakatiyas, Qutb Shahis, and Asaf Jahis (Nizams of Hyderabad). It was historically known as a center for trade, art, and architecture.

Economy

Hyderabad has emerged as a major hub for information technology (IT) and biotechnology industries, earning it the nickname "Cyberabad". It is home to several multinational IT companies, research institutions, and business parks.

Landmarks

The city is dotted with numerous historical landmarks and architectural marvels, including the Charminar (a monument and mosque), Golconda Fort, Qutb Shahi Tombs, and the Chowmahalla Palace.

Education

Hyderabad is home to several esteemed educational institutions, including the University of Hyderabad, Indian School of Business (ISB), and International Institute of Information Technology (IIIT)

Transportation

The city has a well-developed transportation infrastructure, including an international airport (Rajiv Gandhi International Airport), railways, and an extensive road network.
